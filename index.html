The Neo-Soul Movement

I've been asked numerous times; what is Neo-Soul? To me, Neo-Soul is a breakout from the tradition soul offerings of the past. When on its ascension, it was an amalgamation of music for the youth. Soul, Jazz, Funk, and Hip-Hop melding together into one vibey catalogue of sounds.

Soulful Beginnings

Neo-Soul's roots stretch back through the history of African American music and the Civil Rights movement. The evolution of music from blues to jazz to soul shows to As all artists are just evolutions of their inspirations, many Neo-Soul artists have a ancesteral blueprint of artists such as Marvin Gaye, Gladys Knight, Stevie Wonder, Miles Davis, Prince; just to name a few.

With soul falling out of favor in popular music, airwaves taken over by hair metal, hip-hop, and funk/disco, the soul community needed a breath of fresh air.

The Come-Up

The actual beginning of Neo-Soul is a grey area due to its multi-genre nature. Historians (me) suspect the begin with the 1995 Debut Album "Brown Sugar" by D'Angelo. There were albums before it, but this "Brown Sugar" brought the sonics and grooves that came to define the genre.

Height of Soul

Late 90's to the early 2000's. Electric Ladies Studio. The collective known as the Soulquarians began recording in the studio together. Renting out multiple floors in the iconinc recording space, the collective featured on defining albums for the genre. This era was the height of the movement. Where the sonics met with the culutre to create a glimpse into perfection.

Memories of the Past Shape the Future

In the new age of streaming, Neo-Soul's influence can be felt throughout the internet. There was a renissance of sorts for Neo-Soul in the 2010's and on. A generation of music lovers were inspired by the heroes of the past. Weaving the soulful and jazzy sounds into modern day music.
